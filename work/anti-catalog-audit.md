# Anti-catalog audit

Run after drafting any part.

Questions:

1. Does the part have one governing thesis?
2. Is the part organized by lifecycle logic, not by source names?
3. Are cases classified by role: anchor, contrast, boundary, moved-out?
4. Does every case advance the governing thesis?
5. Are there too many equal-level case subsections?
6. Are source facts integrated into argument rather than stacked as notes?
7. Does the part transition naturally from previous lifecycle step to next?
8. Would a reader understand why this part exists without reading the source map?
9. Is any weak case occupying anchor-like space?
10. Is anything better moved to source map, Handbook or Fieldbook?

Output:

- `reports/<part>_ANTI_CATALOG_AUDIT.md`
- clear pass/fail/repair recommendations.
